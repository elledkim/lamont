Establishment of Soil & Forest Monitoring (2024)

In the spring of 2024, the Lamont Sanctuary Forest entered a new era of research and education through the formal establishment of its soil and forest monitoring program. This milestone marked the beginning of a long-term initiative aimed at investigating carbon sequestration, ecological health, and climate resilience within a rewilded forest ecosystem.

Spearheaded by Dr. Yushu Xia and a team of Lamont-Doherty Earth Observatory researchers, the project installed a permanent 1-hectare study plot to collect high-resolution data on both aboveground and belowground systems. This initiative expanded the forest’s research scope far beyond observational ecology to include empirical soil carbon sampling, greenhouse gas (GHG) flux monitoring, and allometric tree measurement.

Throughout the summer of 2024, students from the Secondary School Field Research Program (SSRFP) and undergraduate internships took part in setting up DSP (Dynamic Soil Property) plots, collecting over 300 soil samples, and installing environmental sensors across topographic and vegetation gradients.

The project’s scientific objectives were threefold:

  • Track long-term carbon dynamics: measuring both organic carbon in soil and biomass in trees.
  • Model spatial variation: using digital soil mapping (DSM) techniques to predict soil properties at multiple scales.
  • Educate the next generation: training students in fieldwork, lab analysis, and digital geospatial methods like Google Earth Engine.

The establishment of this program reflects a commitment not only to climate and ecological research, but also to hands-on, interdisciplinary education. Today, Lamont Sanctuary Forest stands as a living laboratory for soil scientists, forest ecologists, and students alike.
